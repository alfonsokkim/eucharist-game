# Brief for Claude (desktop): build presentation slides for "Recreate the Mass"

Paste everything below into Claude on the desktop app and ask it to generate the
slide deck. It is self-contained.

---

**Your task:** Create a presentation slide deck for a high-school faith-formation
lesson on the **Liturgy of the Eucharist**. The deck accompanies a live classroom
game called *Recreate the Mass* in which students vote, step by step, on the
correct order of the Mass. The slides should teach the same 14 steps in the same
order, so the lesson and the game line up exactly.

**Audience:** High-school students (grades 9–12), Catholic faith formation.
Reverent but engaging; clear, not academic.

**Visual style (match the game):** deep liturgical violet (#33264D / #2A1F40)
backgrounds, claret accent (#9C3A4E), gold accent (#D8B46A), off-white text,
large readable type (it's projected). A clean, slightly celebratory feel. A
small pixel/retro game motif is welcome since the companion game is pixel-art.

**Deck structure to produce:**
1. **Title slide** — "The Liturgy of the Eucharist" + subtitle "Recreate the Mass."
2. **What is the Liturgy of the Eucharist?** — one slide: it's the second main
   part of the Mass (after the Liturgy of the Word), where bread and wine become
   the Body and Blood of Christ and are shared in Communion. Note the three
   movements: Preparation of the Gifts → Eucharistic Prayer → Communion Rite.
3. **One slide per step (14 slides)** — for each step below show: the step name,
   what the priest/assembly does, and a one-line "why it matters." Keep each
   slide to a heading + 2–4 short lines. Optionally add a "common mix-up" line
   using the misconception noted.
4. **Recap slide** — the 14 steps as an ordered list (the quick-recall list).
5. **Closing slide** — tie back to the game: "Now recreate the Mass together,"
   and a line that Communion completes the Liturgy of the Eucharist; Mass ends
   with the Concluding Rites.

**The 14 steps, in order (authoritative — do not reorder):**

1. **Preparation of the Gifts** — The priest prepares the altar and receives the
   bread and wine brought forward. *Why:* the Liturgy of the Word has ended; the
   gifts are presented. *Mix-up:* this is not where the Gospel is read (that was
   the Liturgy of the Word).
2. **Offering the Bread** — The priest lifts the bread: "Blessed are you, Lord God
   of all creation." *Why:* the bread is offered first, then the wine. *Mix-up:*
   the collection is part of our offering but is never raised up for worship.
3. **Preparing the Chalice** — Wine is poured into the chalice with a little water.
   *Why:* the mingling signifies Christ's divinity joined to our humanity.
4. **Washing of Hands (Lavabo)** — The priest washes his hands: "Wash me, O Lord,
   from my iniquity." *Why:* a sign of the desire for inner purity before the
   Eucharistic Prayer.
5. **Invitation to Pray (Orate fratres)** — "Pray, brothers and sisters, that my
   sacrifice and yours may be acceptable to God." *Why:* invites the whole
   assembly to offer the sacrifice together. *Mix-up:* "Go in peace" is the
   dismissal at the very end.
6. **The Eucharistic Prayer Begins (Preface Dialogue)** — "The Lord be with you…
   Lift up your hearts… Let us give thanks to the Lord our God." *Why:* it lifts
   our hearts into thanksgiving — the meaning of "Eucharist." *Mix-up:* the
   Confiteor ("I confess…") belongs near the start of Mass.
7. **Holy, Holy, Holy (Sanctus)** — The whole assembly sings "Holy, Holy, Holy
   Lord God of hosts." *Why:* joins our voices to the angels in heaven
   (Isaiah 6:3): "Hosanna in the highest!" *Mix-up:* the Gloria is near the start;
   the Lamb of God comes later.
8. **Calling the Holy Spirit (Epiclesis)** — The priest extends his hands over the
   gifts, calling down the Holy Spirit to consecrate them. *Why:* asks the Father
   to send the Spirit so the gifts become the Body and Blood of Christ.
9. **The Consecration** — The priest speaks Jesus' own words: "This is my Body…"
   and "This is the chalice of my Blood…" *Why:* by Christ's words, spoken by the
   priest in the person of Christ, bread and wine become his Body and Blood —
   **transubstantiation**. (This is the central, most sacred moment.)
10. **The Mystery of Faith** *(optional step)* — The priest proclaims "The mystery
    of faith"; the people respond with the Memorial Acclamation. *Why:* we
    proclaim Christ's death and resurrection; the prayer then closes with the
    great "Amen."
11. **The Our Father** — The Communion Rite begins with the Lord's Prayer. *Why:*
    Jesus' own prayer; "give us this day our daily bread" points to the Eucharist.
12. **The Sign of Peace** — All share a sign of Christ's peace. *Why:* a sign that
    we are one community and all children of God.
13. **The Breaking of the Bread (Fraction)** — The priest breaks the consecrated
    bread as the "Lamb of God" is sung. *Why:* this action gave the earliest
    Church one of its names for the Eucharist.
14. **Holy Communion** — The priest gives the host: "The Body of Christ," and each
    person answers "Amen." *Why:* "Amen" means "I believe." The Liturgy of the
    Eucharist is now complete; Mass closes with the Concluding Rites.

**Output:** a slide deck (e.g., as a slide-by-slide outline I can drop into
Google Slides/PowerPoint, or generated slides if you can). Keep wording reverent
and accurate; do not soften the "mix-ups" into being also-correct — they are
deliberate teaching contrasts.
