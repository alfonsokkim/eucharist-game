# Answer Key - Recreate the Mass

The correct order of the **Liturgy of the Eucharist** as played in the game.
Order follows the Order of Mass (USCCB) and the General Instruction of the Roman
Missal. On each phone the three cards are shuffled, so positions vary; the
*text* below is the correct choice. The two wrong cards in the game are
deliberately plausible (things that belong elsewhere in the Mass); that is the
teaching point.

| # | Step | Question shown to students | Correct answer | Why |
|---|------|----------------------------|----------------|-----|
| 1 | **Preparation of the Gifts** | The Liturgy of the Word has just ended. How does the priest begin the Liturgy of the Eucharist? | Prepare the altar and welcome the bread and wine brought forward | The altar is prepared and the gifts of bread and wine are brought up. The Gospel belongs to the Liturgy of the Word, which is already finished. |
| 2 | **Offering the Bread** | The gifts reach the altar. What does the priest lift up and bless first? | The bread, saying "Blessed are you, Lord God of all creation" | The bread is offered first, then the wine. The collection is part of our offering but is never raised up for worship. |
| 3 | **Preparing the Chalice** | The priest prepares the chalice. What goes into it? | Wine, with a small amount of water added | The mingling of water and wine signifies Christ's divinity joined to our humanity. |
| 4 | **Washing of Hands (Lavabo)** | Before the great prayer begins, what does the priest do at the side of the altar? | Washes his hands, praying to be cleansed of sin | "Wash me, O Lord, from my iniquity." A sign of the desire for inner purity before the Eucharistic Prayer. |
| 5 | **Invitation to Pray (Orate fratres)** | The gifts are ready. How does the priest invite everyone into the sacrifice? | "Pray, brothers and sisters, that my sacrifice and yours may be acceptable to God" | Invites the whole assembly to offer the sacrifice together. ("Go in peace" is the dismissal; the mystery of faith comes later.) |
| 6 | **The Eucharistic Prayer Begins (Preface dialogue)** | The Eucharistic Prayer begins. How does the priest open it? | "The Lord be with you... Lift up your hearts... Let us give thanks to the Lord our God" | The Preface dialogue lifts our hearts into thanksgiving, which is what "Eucharist" means. (The Confiteor belongs near the start of Mass.) |
| 7 | **Holy, Holy, Holy (Sanctus)** | At the end of the Preface, what does everyone sing together? | "Holy, Holy, Holy Lord God of hosts" | Joins our voices to the angels in heaven (Isaiah 6:3): "Hosanna in the highest!" |
| 8 | **Calling the Holy Spirit (Epiclesis)** | The priest holds his hands out over the bread and wine. What is he doing? | Calling down the Holy Spirit to consecrate the gifts | The Epiclesis asks the Father to send the Holy Spirit so the gifts may become the Body and Blood of Christ. |
| 9 | **The Consecration** | The most sacred moment. What words does the priest speak over the bread and wine? | Jesus' own words: "This is my Body..." and "This is the chalice of my Blood..." | By Christ's own words, spoken by the priest in the person of Christ, the bread and wine become his Body and Blood. We call this transubstantiation. |
| 10 | **The Mystery of Faith** *(optional step)* | Right after the consecration, the priest proclaims... | "The mystery of faith," and the people respond with the Memorial Acclamation | We proclaim Christ's death and resurrection; the Eucharistic Prayer then closes with the great "Amen." |
| 11 | **The Our Father** | The Communion Rite begins. Which prayer comes first? | The Our Father (the Lord's Prayer) | Jesus' own prayer. "Give us this day our daily bread" points to the Eucharist we are about to receive. |
| 12 | **The Sign of Peace** | Before Communion, what does the priest invite everyone to share? | A sign of peace with one another | We share Christ's peace, a sign that we are one community and all children of God. |
| 13 | **The Breaking of the Bread (Fraction)** | What does the priest do as the "Lamb of God" is sung? | Breaks the consecrated bread | The breaking of the bread gave the earliest Church one of its names for the Eucharist. |
| 14 | **Holy Communion** | The people come forward. What does the priest say as he gives each person the host? | "The Body of Christ," and the person answers "Amen" | "Amen" means "I believe" that this truly is the Body of Christ. The Liturgy of the Eucharist is now complete; Mass closes with the Concluding Rites. |

**Quick recall list (correct order):**
Preparation of the Gifts, Offering the Bread, Preparing the Chalice,
Washing of Hands, Invitation to Pray, Preface Dialogue, Sanctus, Epiclesis,
Consecration, Mystery of Faith, Our Father, Sign of Peace, Fraction,
Holy Communion.
