# Answer Key — Recreate the Mass

The correct order of the **Liturgy of the Eucharist** as played in the game.
Order follows the Order of Mass (USCCB) and the General Instruction of the Roman
Missal. On each phone the three cards are shuffled, so positions vary — the
*text* below is the correct choice. The two wrong cards in the game are
deliberately plausible (things that belong elsewhere in the Mass); that's the
teaching point.

| # | Step | Correct answer | Why |
|---|------|----------------|-----|
| 1 | **Preparation of the Gifts** | Prepare the altar and welcome the bread and wine brought forward | The altar is prepared and the gifts of bread and wine are brought up. The Gospel belongs to the Liturgy of the Word, already finished. |
| 2 | **Offering the Bread** | The bread, saying "Blessed are you, Lord God of all creation" | The bread is offered first, then the wine. The collection is part of our offering but is never raised up for worship. |
| 3 | **Preparing the Chalice** | Wine, with a small amount of water added | The mingling of water and wine signifies Christ's divinity joined to our humanity. |
| 4 | **Washing of Hands (Lavabo)** | Washes his hands, praying to be cleansed of sin | "Wash me, O Lord, from my iniquity." A sign of desire for inner purity before the Eucharistic Prayer. |
| 5 | **Invitation to Pray (Orate fratres)** | "Pray, brothers and sisters, that my sacrifice and yours may be acceptable to God" | Invites the whole assembly to offer the sacrifice together. ("Go in peace" is the dismissal; the mystery of faith comes later.) |
| 6 | **The Eucharistic Prayer Begins (Preface dialogue)** | "The Lord be with you… Lift up your hearts… Let us give thanks to the Lord our God" | The Preface dialogue lifts our hearts into thanksgiving — which is what "Eucharist" means. (The Confiteor belongs near the start of Mass.) |
| 7 | **Holy, Holy, Holy (Sanctus)** | "Holy, Holy, Holy Lord God of hosts" | Joins our voices to the angels in heaven (Isaiah 6:3): "Hosanna in the highest!" |
| 8 | **Calling the Holy Spirit (Epiclesis)** | Calling down the Holy Spirit to consecrate the gifts | The Epiclesis asks the Father to send the Holy Spirit so the gifts may become the Body and Blood of Christ. |
| 9 | **The Consecration** | Jesus' own words: "This is my Body…" and "This is the chalice of my Blood…" | By Christ's own words, spoken by the priest in the person of Christ, the bread and wine become his Body and Blood — **transubstantiation**. |
| 10 | **The Mystery of Faith** *(optional step)* | "The mystery of faith," and the people respond with the Memorial Acclamation | We proclaim Christ's death and resurrection; the Eucharistic Prayer then closes with the great "Amen." |
| 11 | **The Our Father** | The Our Father (the Lord's Prayer) | Jesus' own prayer. "Give us this day our daily bread" points to the Eucharist we are about to receive. |
| 12 | **The Sign of Peace** | A sign of peace with one another | We share Christ's peace — a sign that we are one community and all children of God. |
| 13 | **The Breaking of the Bread (Fraction)** | Breaks the consecrated bread | The breaking of the bread gave the earliest Church one of its names for the Eucharist. |
| 14 | **Holy Communion** | "The Body of Christ," and the person answers "Amen" | "Amen" means "I believe" that this truly is the Body of Christ. The Liturgy of the Eucharist is now complete; Mass closes with the Concluding Rites. |

**Quick recall list (correct order):**
Preparation of the Gifts → Offering the Bread → Preparing the Chalice →
Washing of Hands → Invitation to Pray → Preface Dialogue → Sanctus → Epiclesis →
Consecration → Mystery of Faith → Our Father → Sign of Peace → Fraction →
Holy Communion.
